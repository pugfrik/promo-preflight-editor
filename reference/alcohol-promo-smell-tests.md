# Alcohol & prize promo smell-tests

Used by the **conditional legal smell-test** lens — it fires only when a
draft touches alcohol pricing/giveaways, prizes, raffles, sweepstakes,
contests, or entry mechanics.

> ⚠ **Epistemic caveat (carried from source):** these smell-tests distill
> a working rule library that is owner-stated / assistant-seeded and
> **not counsel-verified**. They exist to FLAG risks, never to clear a
> promo. The editor's verdict on any match is always: name the risk, cite
> the source tag, and say **"verify with counsel before running this."**
> Alcohol law also varies by state and county — these tests were written
> against New York-shaped rules; treat them as the *kind* of trap to look
> for, not the law of any reader's jurisdiction.

## The smell-tests

| Trigger phrasing in a draft | What to flag | Source tag |
|---|---|---|
| Alcohol discounted more than ~half price; discounts stacking on a happy-hour price; "half off drinks!" plus anything | Discount-depth ceilings exist for alcohol; stacked promos silently cross them; bundles mixing alcohol + non-alcohol make the discount's legal target ambiguous | NY-ABC-01 |
| "Free drink/pint/shot", free drink WITH purchase, alcohol as a prize, bonus, or bundled freebie, "complimentary welcome pour" at a paid event | Free alcohol is broadly barred; "free with purchase" is still free alcohol; alcohol prizes double-trip consumption-incentive scrutiny. Giveaway mutations should substitute space, merch, experiences, or services — never the drink | NY-ABC-02 |
| Dice rolls, spin-to-win, raffle tickets sold with drinks, drink-count contests, "finish the flight" challenges | Gambling-flavored mechanics and consumption incentives draw scrutiny even where technically arguable; prizes whose value scales with drinking are consumption incentives | NY-ABC-03 |
| "Bottomless", open bar per-head, all-you-can-drink, flat-fee party where guests drink freely, unlimited "tasting pass" | One-price-unmetered-alcohol formats are restricted; the trap catches formats that don't look like AYCD. Metered per-drink pricing or alcohol-excluded flat fees are the clean shapes | NY-ABC-04 |
| Prize/giveaway described without excluding alcohol; gift cards or house credit as prizes | Prize inventory should be explicitly non-alcohol (space, merch, tours, experiences); gift cards redeemable against alcohol are effectively discounted/free alcohol | NY-ABC-05 |
| "Every purchase is an entry", buy/pay/book to enter a drawing, entry requires a visit, mandatory posting/tagging to enter | Prize + chance + consideration = illegal lottery. Consideration is broader than money (visits, posts, surveys have been argued). Clean shape: genuinely free entry, open to non-customers, equal odds | US-SWEEP-01 |
| Purchase-linked entries alongside a "free way to enter" | The free path (AMOE) must be disclosed, practical, and EQUAL in odds/value — a buried or weaker free path does not cure consideration | US-SWEEP-02 |
| Public prize drawing with no official rules mentioned; ads without "no purchase necessary"; no age eligibility stated | Sweepstakes need published official rules (sponsor, eligibility, entry period/method, odds, prize + value, selection). At an alcohol venue, 21+ eligibility even when the prize excludes alcohol. Rarely kills a promo — it produces a requirements list | US-SWEEP-03 |
| Early openings, late-night events, extended hours, promoting a specific service window | Permitted serving hours depend on license class and county rules (Sundays/holidays may differ); "last call" practice is not the legal boundary. Any promo extending or promoting service hours is a verify item | LOC-01 |

## How to use a match

1. Quote the draft's own words that tripped the test.
2. Say what the trap is, in one or two sentences, plainly.
3. Cite the source tag (e.g., "NY-ABC-02 smell").
4. Close: **"verify with counsel before running this."** Never "this is
   illegal" — and never "this is fine."
