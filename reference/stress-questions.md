# Stress questions — the operator interview

Used by the **Margin** and **Execution** lenses. Questions a promo must
survive before it runs; the operator's answers are the data.

## At intake (before the promo is designed further)

- **Which master does this serve?** Every offer has a primary beneficiary:
  the customer's desire, or the business's need (content, leads, cash
  timing). Name it. If an offer component serves the business more than
  the customer (canonical case: a "free photo package" prize — the venue
  wants promo shots; the customer wants nice photos OF THEMSELVES), the
  messaging must sell the customer's version, and the business benefit
  rides along contractually (usage rights), not as the pitch.
- **What are you trying to achieve?** New customers, repeat customers,
  fast cash, capacity fill, lead generation, content generation? A promo
  that can't name its goal can't be measured against one.

## At scoring (before a promo is marked ready to run)

- **Margin at scale:** if this offer is massively successful — say 100
  more bookings — does the unit margin fund the team you'd need to hire
  and train to execute them?
- **Staffing reality:** who runs this, concretely, on a Tuesday? If the
  answer is "the owner, at 11pm," the offer fails on ease regardless of
  its other scores.
- **Downside cap:** what's the worst-case cost if redemption is 5× the
  estimate? Is it capped by design (fixed prize count, off-hours only)?
- **Lead capture:** does every non-winner/non-buyer leave contactable?
  (If not, the offer is leaving its cheapest asset on the table.)

## Canonical two-masters case (for calibration)

A taproom's party-giveaway prize originally read "free photo package" —
motivated by the venue's need for party/space content. Risk: weak as an
enticement. Refined shape: **party + photo mini-session** — posed shots
(mostly for the winner; mini-sessions are independently popular) + candid
party shots (mostly for the venue), with a contractual agreement for the
venue's promotional use. Same cost, both masters served, honest messaging.
