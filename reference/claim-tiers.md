# Claim tiers — what a run length can honestly earn

Used by the **Measurability** lens. Every success target should be written
as the marketable claim it would earn — and the claim's tier must match
the run's length and sample size. Flag any target written above its tier.

## The three tiers

| Tier | Example claim | Earnable in |
|---|---|---|
| **Visit** | "Sold 20+ pints over an average Friday" | days |
| **Period** | "2x'd Tuesday covers this month" | weeks–a month |
| **Business** | "Grew monthly revenue 50%", "10x'd weekend sales" | a season or more |

The rule: **refuse business-tier claims on short runs.** A two-weekend
promo cannot prove a business transformed; it can prove what those
weekends did against a matched baseline.

## Worked examples — rewriting a target at the tier it can earn

- ❌ "This promo will 10x our weekend sales" (business tier, 2-weekend
  run) → ✅ "Weekend covers up ≥25% vs the trailing 8-week same-weekday
  baseline during the promo window" (visit/period tier).
- ❌ "Trivia night transformed our Tuesdays" (business tier, 4 runs) →
  ✅ "Tuesday gross up ≥30% on the four trivia nights vs the prior 8
  Tuesdays" (period tier).
- ❌ "The loyalty push grew our customer base" (business tier, 1 month) →
  ✅ "120 new loyalty sign-ups in 30 days, ≥40% of them making a second
  visit within the window" (period tier).
